Details About
the Planets
A novel way to visualize our solar sytem. As the means to visualize data progresses, old ways of viewing data should be updated. The Planets provides a new way to see our solar sytem using modern techniques.
An experiment with WebVR and 360° technologies. The Planets was built using Mozilla's AFrame: a framework for building WebVR experiences. All of the code is open source and can be found here.
How to View View the solar system the way you want to.
Desktop 360° To view the Planets on your desktop, simply click the link to enter the deck. Then using your mouse, click and drag to change your scope of view.

Click events are "gaze-based". This means that you will have to position the yellow cursor in the middle of the screen over the button you would like to select and trigger the selection.
Mobile Gyro-Based Enter the deck through a browser (Chrome and Firefox recommended) on your phone. In the bottom right corner, you will see a button with the outline of a VR headset. Click this button. Your phone will enter the VR mode. Next put your phone into the headset container of your choice.

Click events are "gaze-based". This means that you will have to position the yellow cursor in the middle of the screen over the button you would like to select and trigger the selection.
Mobile VR Enter the deck through a browser (Chrome and Firefox recommended) on your phone. Once you are in the deck, your phone will track its position and correlate it with the controls in the browser.

Click events are "gaze-based". This means that you will have to position the yellow cursor in the middle of the screen over the button you would like to select and trigger the selection.
